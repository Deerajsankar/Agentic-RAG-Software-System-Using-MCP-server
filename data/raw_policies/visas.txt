# ExampleCorp Immigration, Visas, and Global Mobility Policy (Enterprise Edition)

**Effective date:** 2026-03-17  
**Document owner:** Global Mobility (People Ops) + Legal (Immigration Counsel)  
**Applies to:** Employees, candidates, interns (where applicable), and managers initiating cross-border moves  

> This policy provides detailed governance for work authorization, international assignments, business travel, and cross-border remote work. It is not legal advice; however, it defines **mandatory company process**. Employees must follow the process even when local law would allow more flexibility, unless the process is legally prohibited.

---

# 1. Core principles and risk categories

## 1.1 Why this policy is strict
Immigration noncompliance can create:
- Fines and sanctions for the company and individual
- Deportation or entry bans
- Tax/payroll violations
- Customer contract breaches (location and export-control clauses)

## 1.2 Definitions
| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Work authorization** | Legal permission to perform work in a jurisdiction |
| **Business travel** | Short-term travel for meetings/training without productive work (definition varies by country) |
| **Assignment** | Temporary relocation with payroll/host entity implications |
| **Cross-border remote work** | Working while physically located outside the employee’s authorized work country |

---

# 2. Pre-approval requirements (non-negotiable)

## 2.1 Activities requiring Mobility approval
Approval is required before:
1. Any cross-border work beyond **10 business days** in a rolling 90-day period
2. Any relocation to a different country (permanent or temporary)
3. Any assignment where payroll may shift or a host entity is involved
4. Any role that touches **export-controlled** technology or restricted customer data in a new location

## 2.2 What is not allowed without exception approval
- Working from sanctioned or embargoed territories
- “Visa runs” or repeated border entries to avoid immigration rules
- Performing productive work on a visitor status where prohibited

---

# 3. Candidate and employee work authorization workflows

## 3.1 Hiring candidates who require sponsorship
Managers must:
- Flag sponsorship needs early in the requisition
- Use approved immigration counsel
- Avoid making timeline promises (visa processing is unpredictable)

**Minimum documentation controls:**
- Identity and qualification documents collected securely
- No emailing passports/IDs to personal emails or unapproved tools
- Case tracking in the Mobility system

## 3.2 Extensions, renewals, and maintenance
Employees must:
- Respond to counsel requests within **5 business days**
- Notify Mobility immediately of:
  - Address changes (where legally required)
  - Passport renewals/expirations
  - Arrests/convictions (where relevant to immigration status)

**Edge case:** If an employee delays providing required documents and status lapses, system access may be suspended until resolved.

---

# 4. Business travel policy (enterprise controls)

## 4.1 Business travel vs. work (practical guidance)
Many countries allow meetings, conferences, or training on visitor status but prohibit hands-on productive work. The line is nuanced.

Examples typically treated as **business travel** (country-dependent):
- Attending meetings, planning sessions, conferences
- Receiving training (not delivering paid services)

Examples likely treated as **work**:
- Hands-on engineering work for a local entity
- On-site client delivery work
- Performing services that displace local labor

## 4.2 Travel pre-check requirements
Before international travel, employees must complete:
1. Mobility pre-check form (destination, dates, purpose)
2. Security review if traveling with Confidential+ data
3. Export control screening if role/project is restricted

## 4.3 High-risk destinations
Some destinations require:
- Burner device policy
- Additional encryption controls
- Pre-approval for carrying certain data or tools

---

# 5. Cross-border remote work (the “digital nomad” problem)

## 5.1 Default rule
Employees may not work outside their authorized work country without Mobility approval.

## 5.2 Common edge cases
1. **“I’m visiting family for 3 weeks and will keep working.”**  
   - Requires approval if it exceeds the threshold or triggers local risk.
2. **“I have citizenship in the destination country.”**  
   - Still requires approval due to payroll/tax/export-control risk.
3. **“I’ll only check email.”**  
   - Even limited work can create a permanent establishment or immigration risk.

## 5.3 Consequences of noncompliance
Violations may result in:
- Mandatory return to home country
- Disciplinary action
- Visa case impact and potential termination (depending on severity)

# 6. Assignments, transfers, and relocation governance

## 6.1 Assignment categories
| Category | Typical duration | Common controls |
|---|---:|---|
| Short-term assignment | 1–6 months | Housing support, limited payroll changes |
| Long-term assignment | 6–24 months | Host-country payroll, tax equalization |
| Permanent transfer | 24+ months | New employment terms, entity transfer |

## 6.2 Cost governance
- All relocation packages require a pre-approved budget.
- Exceptions require VP-level approval plus Finance signoff.

## 6.3 Repayment agreements (clawbacks)
Relocation benefits may be subject to repayment if the employee resigns within a defined period.

| Benefit type | Repayment window | Typical repayment method |
|---|---:|---|
| Relocation lump sum | 12 months | Pro-rated payroll deduction |
| Immigration fees | 24 months (where lawful) | Pro-rated repayment |

**Edge case:** Repayment clauses must comply with local labor law; some jurisdictions restrict deductions.

---

# 7. Data protection, export controls, and access limitations

## 7.1 Data handling while traveling
- Do not access HR or Highly Confidential systems from unsecured networks without VPN and device compliance.
- Use minimum data; avoid downloading large datasets.

## 7.2 Export control screening
Some projects/technologies cannot be accessed from certain locations or by certain individuals.
- Mobility + Legal + Security will determine restrictions.
- Attempts to bypass geo-controls are a policy violation.

---

# 8. Roles and responsibilities

## 8.1 Employee responsibilities
- Provide truthful information and complete requested forms promptly
- Maintain valid travel documents
- Follow approved itineraries for work-related travel

## 8.2 Manager responsibilities
- Avoid informal approvals (“sure, just work from there”)
- Plan staffing to account for immigration timelines
- Escalate high-risk travel or relocation requests early

## 8.3 Mobility and Legal responsibilities
- Maintain approved counsel relationships
- Track cases, deadlines, and compliance evidence
- Provide documented guidance and approvals/denials
