# ExampleCorp Manager Playbook (Enterprise Edition)

**Effective date:** 2026-03-17  
**Document owner:** People Ops (HRBP / Employee Relations)  
**Audience:** People managers, senior leaders, HR partners, program managers supporting people processes  

> This playbook standardizes management behavior for consistency, auditability, and risk reduction. It intentionally includes edge cases, “what-if” scenarios, and procedural controls to support enterprise-grade RAG testing.

---

# 1. Manager role charter (what you are accountable for)

Managers at ExampleCorp are accountable for **business outcomes** and **people outcomes**. This includes:
- **Goal clarity:** Translating strategy into measurable objectives (team and individual).
- **Talent stewardship:** Hiring, onboarding, coaching, performance management, succession planning.
- **Compliance posture:** Applying policies, protecting HR data, and escalating appropriately.
- **Operational hygiene:** Timekeeping integrity (where applicable), budgeting inputs, tooling compliance.

---

# 2. Operating cadence and documentation rules

## 2.1 Required rituals (minimum standard)
1. **1:1 meetings**: At least biweekly; weekly for new hires and critical roles.
2. **Team meetings**: Weekly with documented decisions and action items.
3. **Quarterly planning**: Goals, staffing plan, risk register, and cross-functional dependencies.

## 2.2 “If it’s not recorded, it didn’t happen”
Managers must maintain documentation in approved systems only:
- Performance notes in the performance system / HRIS module
- Hiring notes in the ATS
- Employee relations issues via People Ops case management

**Strict prohibition:** Do not keep performance dossiers in personal files, shared drives, or personal cloud storage.

---

# 3. Hiring and onboarding (risk-controlled approach)

## 3.1 Structured hiring
- Use role scorecards with competencies and evidence.
- Avoid informal “culture fit” language; assess role-relevant behaviors.
- Never request prohibited information (health, family planning, protected characteristics).

## 3.2 Offer governance
- Compensation offers must be within approved bands and follow pay equity rules.
- Exceptions require written justification, HRBP approval, and, where applicable, Finance approval.

## 3.3 Onboarding checklist (first 30 days)
- [ ] Access provisioned via ticketing with least privilege
- [ ] Role expectations and success metrics documented
- [ ] First project scoped with milestones
- [ ] Compliance training completed (security, privacy, ethics)

---

# 4. Performance management (enterprise-grade)

## 4.1 Goals and metrics
Goals should be:
- **Specific** (what will be delivered)
- **Measurable** (how success is evaluated)
- **Time-bound** (when)
- **Risk-aware** (dependencies and assumptions)

## 4.2 Feedback standards
Use behavior-and-impact framing:
1. Situation/Context
2. Observable behavior
3. Impact on team/customer
4. Expected alternative
5. Follow-up date

**Edge case:** If the employee disputes facts, document both perspectives and seek corroboration (tickets, PRs, incident logs).

## 4.3 Calibration and fairness controls
Managers must:
- Participate in calibration sessions
- Use evidence, not popularity
- Avoid surprise negative ratings (unless misconduct or sudden change)

## 4.4 Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)
PIPs are used when sustained performance gaps exist and coaching has not closed them.

### Minimum PIP contents
| Section | Required elements |
|---|---|
| Performance gaps | Specific examples + evidence |
| Expectations | Measurable targets and due dates |
| Support plan | Training, pairing, check-ins |
| Review cadence | Weekly checkpoints (minimum) |
| Outcomes | Success criteria + consequences |

**Strict rules:**
- Coordinate with HRBP before initiating a PIP.
- Do not make promises about outcomes; follow process and evidence.
- If the employee requests accommodations, pause and involve People Ops to evaluate under applicable law.

# 5. Employee Relations (ER): escalation and confidentiality

## 5.1 What must be escalated immediately
Escalate to People Ops/ER within **24 hours**:
- Harassment/discrimination allegations
- Threats/violence/safety concerns
- Suspected fraud, time theft (where relevant), or retaliation
- High-risk data/privacy incidents involving employee information

## 5.2 Confidentiality boundaries
- Share information strictly on a need-to-know basis.
- Do not discuss investigations with the team.
- Avoid “reassurance by oversharing” (common compliance failure).

---

# 6. Compensation, promotions, and sensitive data

## 6.1 Compensation confidentiality
- Compensation details are Highly Confidential.
- Discuss compensation only with:
  - The employee (their own comp)
  - HR/Finance as authorized
  - Approvers in the workflow

**Edge case:** If an employee shares compensation information voluntarily, managers should not discourage lawful discussion where protected by local law, but must still avoid disclosing others’ comp.

## 6.2 Promotions
Promotions are not “rewards”; they reflect sustained operation at the next level.

### Promotion packet (typical)
1. Scope and impact evidence
2. Competency mapping
3. Peer/partner feedback (structured)
4. Business need and backfill impact

---

# 7. Leave, attendance, and wellbeing

## 7.1 Leave administration
Managers approve time off in HRIS but should not request medical details.

## 7.2 Workload risk and burnout controls
Managers must monitor:
- Repeated after-hours work
- On-call load
- Incident frequency and recovery time

Mitigations include:
- Rotations, staffing adjustments, and enforced cooldowns after critical incidents.

---

# 8. Managing across regions (apply the correct rule set)

Managers must apply policies based on the employee’s **work location** and **legal entity**, not the manager’s location.

Examples of region-sensitive areas:
- Termination notice and severance requirements
- Working time regulations and overtime eligibility
- Works council consultation (e.g., Germany)
- Data privacy constraints (GDPR and local rules)

**Strict rule:** Do not improvise region-specific HR guidance. Escalate to People Ops.

---

# 9. Common “what-if” scenarios (edge cases)

1. **Employee is remote from another country temporarily**  
   - Escalate to Mobility; do not approve informally.
2. **Employee performance issue overlaps with medical leave**  
   - Pause and involve HRBP; handle accommodations appropriately.
3. **Team member reports harassment but asks you to “do nothing”**  
   - You must still escalate; confidentiality will be protected as feasible.
4. **Employee asks for off-cycle promotion/raise due to external offer**  
   - Follow retention policy; coordinate with HR/Finance; do not promise outcomes.

- [ ] Escalate ER concerns within required timelines
- [ ] Protect HR data; avoid personal storage
- [ ] Apply region-specific policies correctly
